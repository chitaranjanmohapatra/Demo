Consumer prices in March 2018: 1.6% rise on March 2017 expected

The inflation rate in Germany as measured by the consumer price index is expected to be 1.6% in March 2018. Based on the results available so far, the Federal Statistical Office (Destatis) also reports that the consumer prices are expected to increase by 0.4% on February 2018.

More : Consumer prices in March 2018: 1.6% rise on March 2017 expected …

Consumer prices in February 2018: +1.4% on February 2017

Consumer prices in Germany were 1.4% higher in February 2018 than in February 2017. The inflation rate – measured by the consumer price index – thus decreased for the third consecutive month. In January 2018, it had been +1.6%. Compared with January 2018, the consumer price index rose by 0.5% in February 2018. The Federal Statistical Office (Destatis) thus confirms its provisional overall results of 27 February 2018. 

More : Consumer prices in February 2018: +1.4% on February 2017 …

Harmonised Consumer Prices in 2017 – Trends in Europe

In 2017, the annual rate of change of the Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices (HICP) for Germany was higher (+1.7%) than the average rate of change for the euro area (+1.5%). The annual inflation rate of the EU as well as of the European Economic Area increased by +1.7% and thus in the same extent as the HICP for Germany. The present flyer gives an overview of consumer price trends in Europe in 2017 as measured by the HICP.
